Recommended actions

  • Prioritize customers who accepted one or more previous campaigns; their historical likelihood of responding again is substantially higher.
  • Within previous acceptors, give greater priority to high and very-high spenders and personalize the offer to their preferred purchase channel.
  • Use web-focused messages for digitally active age segments and store or catalogue offers where those channels show stronger purchase activity.
  • Use age and income only to refine the audience, not as stand-alone targeting rules.
  • Test the priority audience against a control group before scaling; track response rate, sales value and campaign cost.

How to use these insights

  • Start with campaign history to identify likely responders.
  • Use the dropdown analysis to compare age, income and spending patterns without filtering the whole dashboard.
  • Use the age selector in the channel chart only when planning channel-specific communication.
  • These are historical propensity insights—not guaranteed predictions or a validated machine-learning model.

Source: cleaned marketing campaign dataset (2,240 customers). No global filters are applied. Previous campaign acceptance means accepting at least one of Campaigns 1–5.
